GGT & AG: Geometric group theory and algebraic geometry

In geometric group theory, questions are 'algebraic'. People wants to know some properties of a group or the structure of a group. The way people get information on a certain group is via a group action on some 'geometric' space that people are more familiar with.

The following diagram illustrates their differences: $$\begin{aligned} \textbf{GGT}: & \text{Algebraic problems} \longrightarrow \text{Geometric models} \longrightarrow \text{Algebraic information} \\ \textbf{AG}: & \text{Geometric problems} \longrightarrow \text{Algebraic objects: rings, sheaves} \longrightarrow \text{Geometric information} \end{aligned}$$
